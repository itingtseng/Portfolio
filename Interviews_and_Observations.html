Interviews and Observations

Our team chose LinkedIn for the task analysis.

Our team chose LinkedIn for the task analysis. We set two scenarios for our users. and the second is: searching and applying jobs on LinkedIn. During our contextual inquiry, we would ask user to update LinkedIn profile. At the same time, we would introduce ourselves, explain project and gather demographics like age, gender, professional/student, job title etc. during the observation. We conducted semi-structured interview and we introduce ourselves, explain project and gather demographics, either. The questions we ask users including why do they use it, when do they use it, where do they use it, what do you like or dislike about it, and what would you change about it. We use pen and paper for recording and we use optional audio backup.

It turn out that LinkedIn users including students and professionals in various corporations and industries. Most users are among the typical employment age range (18-67), and most of our users are using LinkedIn when they are at a transition point between careers. The skills to perform tasks within LinkedIn varies depending on how long and what frequency they use LinkedIn.

In addition, there is no typical environment that users use LinkedIn, and the device users like to use LinkedIn is not specific to mobil phone or laptop, neither.

My teammate and I discussed the observation protocol and interview protocol together. After we gathering our data, we use affinity diagraming for qualitative research analysis. We put ideas on sticky notes and grouped them.

Many users mentioned about the problem when they tried to login LinkedIn, and they also mentioned about the premium account issue. After we creating affinity diagraming, we discussed two representative persona for LinkedIn users.

I know the process of analysis qualitative data during the exercise. By compiling data (our sticky notes), we understood that it was not easy to share data with team. Because everyone has different opinion for what should put down in the sticky notes. Some teammate thought that we should write down the demographic for our users, and some teammate thought that we should write user’s action that they have behaved during the observation or interviewing. After all our teammates have placed all that data on the white board, there was another problem. We all have various comments for how to group these sticky notes. One of my teammate said that we should based on the questions that we asked them, and the other teammate stressed that it should be according to the process we conducted in the contextual inquiry or interview. We have spent lots of time communicating about it. However, we all have a consensus and then we can draw the connections across the data and look for patterns. We identified overall trends by interpreting finding in our affinity diagramming.
