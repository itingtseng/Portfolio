iPlants

Integration Platform

Abstract

The development of Taiwan's smart agriculture goes one step further. Taiwan Agricultural Research Institute has developed the i-plants field cultivation management system, combining geographic information system with aerial photography, Internet of Things, and agricultural big data. Not only providing information about crops growing, but also processing and packaging to consumers. Farmers using i-plants can also receive early warning notification of plant diseases and insect pests. The goal is to schedule cultivation plan in advance and improve crop quality.

Goal

“i-Plants is not only a service for consumers, but also for farmers.” i-Plants started research and development in 2015. After two years of testing, it is fully developed and will be released to the market. The target users are agriculture production&marketing groups and agribusiness. The goal is to accelerate smart agriculture in Taiwan.

Ideation

i-Plants records crops growing status with the field recording APP. It also combines agricultural big data to let farmers know the right time for application of pesticide or fertilizer and harvest. Usually, crop pests would break out when accumulate to a certain extent, and Taiwan Agricultural Research Institute has monitored these data every week. Before the outbreak, i-Plants will inform the farmer, so that they can prevent early without self evaluation. Because there is a database for reference, it can also assist novice farmers for farming.

Dataset

i-Plants is connected to the soil database of the Taiwan Agricultural Research Institute, so it can examine every farmland that users want to see. "If it is not suitable for growing corn, we will advise farmers to switch to other crops." In addition, through i-plants, farmers can also be notified how long it will be harvested so that farmers can prepare early.

  • Concept Map

    In the initial of the product development, we write down every data we can interface and goal for each roles. Try to find out our features and market segmentation. By drawing affinity diagram, we understand the relationship between every data and then we can decide which data should we need.

  • Process Flow Diagram

    After we figure out the whole database then we can start to think about the process of data and how can we manage or apply them. We can realize how these data impact users and connect them to the needs of users. Then we can make a contrast with the system which is we trying to improve.

  • Ecosytem Diagram

    In this process, we visualize every role in our ecosystem. Because the whole product impact not only farm, agribusiness, and consumer, as well as the certification body and package factory. Illustrating the conceptual relationship between various system touchpoints, therefore, the product is gradually formed.

  • Storyboarding

    After the analysis of data, it is time to design. We disassemble each user in our ecosystem and try to research the contextual of using. By implementing out research, we using the storyboarding to make sure our user want to use it. This is one of the example that I try to use game interface to attract our farmer to record cultivation.

Outcomes for Farmer

Our client Great Agriculture Co., Ltd. started to use i-Plants for managing corn production last year. Its corn production area is more than 500 hectares with a total of more than 100 farmers. In order to use i-Plants system, they all equipped with a tablet, not only to manage diseases and insect pests early, but also manage soil condition and achieve quality control.

The record data using i-Plants to record can not be changed once uploaded, which is usually written with paper and pen nowadays. i-Plant can be plugged in with further function like order system, and become a one-stop service.

Outcomes for Comuser

Consumers can see the whole growth process of crops by scanning into i-plants via mobile phones. Not only recoding the photos from seedlings to the harvest, i-Plants also records the climate of whole growth process.

Many of consumers eat corn but don't know what corn looks like. The traceability system in i-Plants are not just a resume. There are photos of growth at each stage of the crop as well as animations. When consumers eat an agricultural product, they can know the growth process of plants through i-Plants, which is significant for food and farming education.
