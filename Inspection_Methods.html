Summary findings for our project

Recommendations and Feedback

Team 7 used heuristic evaluation methods for usability testing on the LinkedIn website for our team. They also used the cognitive walkthrough method with the persona Sam. Their findings are below.

By identifying problems, they then conclude these problems and provide relevant suggestions for heuristic evaluation. It is an easy way to evaluate the process the whole picture of the website, however, they also felt this way has some drawbacks:

1. Require a certain level of knowledge and experience to apply

Linkedin and Canvas are both websites which we are really familiar with. Therefore, we spent less time getting familiar with it. However, aside from websites that we use on a daily basis, it takes time to realize the main problem for the system. Therefore, the familiarity could be an issue for conducting a heuristic evaluation.

2. Does not allow to assess the quality of redesign

Though rating the severity of these problems, there are few practical redesign ideas we could provide by using heuristic analysis. It takes extra effort to present our redesign ideas at this time.

Prioritized Issues
More flexible editing environment for updating profile

To make the editing environment more friendly for our users, the system should allow users to reorder their records, and to preview their profiles by easily switching between editor and viewer modes.

Personalize job searching experience

To optimize the job searching experience, which is the major experience for users, the system needs to provide an intuitive button for the job-saving list, and the suggested keywords to allow users do more relevant searching by providing additional keywords.

Revise inconsistency on profile editing

The current website has lots of inconsistency, including update background picture, minimize drop-down box and so on. We suggest to list down the all the inconsistency and compile them by navigating different user flows and scenarios.

Suggestions For Our Task Analysis and Personas

1. Avoid identifying the skilled users as the persona in IT, CS, ME field. Try to identify problems for the unskilled group of people.

2. Select new-beginners as typical users for primary persona could be more valuable.

The scenarios within task analysis do consider the difficulties for users.

Our Response to Suggestions

It would be reasonable to create a persona that is a new LinkedIn user, and for a full in-depth study we would definitely include such a persona. However, we believe that Patricia serves a similar purpose while also being more representative of the LinkedIn user population. Regarding suggestion #1, Patricia is not technically savvy. And regarding suggestion #2, Patricia has minimal familiarity with the site because she rarely uses it. This trait provides a framework to investigate issues of both learnability and memorability.
